‚ö†Ô∏è Camp Pendleton Instructions

CRITICAL: Choose Your Route

You have two options through Camp Pendleton. Decide in advance and prepare accordingly.

Option 1: Through Base (Preferred)

  • Pre-register required: Get a cycling pass from Main Gate Visitor Center (Oceanside side)
  • Bring Real ID - driver's license, state ID, or passport
  • Pass is free, valid for 1 year
  • Uses old Route 101 - scenic, 11 miles of bike-only road
  • Goes through San Onofre beach area - beautiful!
  • Check for closures: Military ops can close the route

Contact: Camp Pendleton Visitor Center at Main Gate

Option 2: I-5 Freeway Shoulder

  • Legal when base is closed to cyclists
  • ~8 miles on I-5 shoulder from Las Pulgas to Oceanside
  • Wide shoulders but watch for debris
  • Traffic is fast and loud - stay focused
  • Single file, high visibility gear recommended
  • Aliso Creek Rest Area has water/restrooms (exit and re-enter)
  • Do not stop on shoulder - keep moving

Note: If base is open, you must use base route or risk a ticket.
