About

I architect clinical data platforms and AI strategies that turn complex healthcare data into meaningful patient outcomes. With 15+ years leading enterprise initiatives across research institutions, health tech, and patient advocacy organizations, I specialize in the intersection of clinical informatics, machine learning, and healthcare operations.

At the Muscular Dystrophy Association, I'm driving a platform transformation that reimagines how patient registries operate: from legacy data collection to AI-powered, EMR-integrated clinical intelligence systems. My work focuses on establishing data governance frameworks, FHIR-based integrations, and scalable infrastructure that other patient advocacy groups can adopt.

Current Focus

  • Patient registry governance and data stewardship for rare neuromuscular diseases
  • EMR-to-EDC integration frameworks and thought leadership for patient advocacy groups
  • Open science initiatives advancing digital biomarker research
  • Enterprise clinical data platform architecture with LLM-powered workflows
  • Strategic partnerships and clinical data commercialization models
